<p><p>Low-Cost Carriers (LCCs) operate in two primary market types: Short-Haul and Line-Haul. Short-Haul markets involve flights typically under 1,000 miles, focusing on quick, frequent, and cost-efficient routes between nearby cities. LCCs optimize for high aircraft utilization and minimal turnaround times. Line-Haul markets cover longer distances, often over 1,000 miles, connecting major hubs or distant regions. Here, LCCs emphasize fuel efficiency, streamlined operations, and competitive pricing to attract budget-conscious travelers while maintaining profitability over extended routes. Both markets prioritize affordability and operational efficiency.</p></p>

<p><p>The stereo audio codecs market comprises two main types: analog and digital. Analog codecs convert sound waves into electrical signals, focusing on traditional audio applications like vinyl records and cassette players. In contrast, digital codecs process audio data as binary information, enhancing sound quality and enabling advanced features like compression and streaming. Digital codecs dominate the market due to their versatility in modern devices, including smartphones and computers, while analog codecs appeal to audiophiles seeking a nostalgic sound experience.</p></p>

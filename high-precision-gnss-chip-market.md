Dual Receiver chips utilize two GNSS receivers to enhance accuracy and reduce positioning errors, making them suitable for applications requiring high precision. In contrast, Multiple Receiver chips incorporate several GNSS receivers, enabling the collection of data across various satellite constellations simultaneously. This configuration offers improved reliability and performance in challenging environments, making them ideal for advanced applications like autonomous vehicles and precision agriculture.</p></p>

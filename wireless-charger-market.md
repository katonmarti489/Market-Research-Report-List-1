<p><p>The wireless charger market encompasses three primary types: Magnetic Resonance, Electromagnetic Induction, and Radio Frequency. </p><p>Magnetic Resonance allows for charging over longer distances with multiple devices simultaneously, utilizing resonant coils. Electromagnetic Induction is the most common method, using close proximity charging through magnetic fields between coils within the charger and the device. </p><p>Radio Frequency technology harnesses ambient radio waves, enabling charging from a distance, although typically at lower power levels. Each type offers unique benefits catering to various user needs and applications.</p></p>
